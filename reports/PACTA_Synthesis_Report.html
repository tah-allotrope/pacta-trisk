2. Methodology & Data Dictionary

This analysis follows the PACTA for Banks Methodology (v1.2.3) developed by RMI, implemented through the open-source pacta.loanbook R package ecosystem.

Pipeline Steps

  1. Data Preparation: Loan book (borrower names, sectors, loan amounts) + ABCD (Asset-Based Company Data) + climate scenarios
  2. Sector Pre-Join: Classify loanbook sectors before matching to enable post-match sector validation
  3. Matching: Fuzzy-match borrower names to ABCD companies (min_score = 0.9), flag scores < 1.0 for manual review, then prioritize
  4. Market Share Approach: For power & automotive — compare portfolio’s weighted technology share against scenario targets
  5. Sectoral Decarbonization Approach (SDA): For cement & steel — compare portfolio’s emission intensity against convergence pathway

10. Vietnam Context & Next Steps

Vietnam-Specific Considerations

  • Sector Classification: Vietnamese banks use VSIC (Vietnam Standard Industrial Classification). A VSIC-to-PACTA sector mapping is needed. Prior VSIC & NAICS mapping work can be leveraged for this.
  • ABCD Data: This is the most challenging input for Vietnamese banks. Asset-level data must typically be purchased from providers like Asset Impact, or self-prepared from public disclosures, industrial registries, and company annual reports.
  • Scenarios: IEA WEO or NGFS scenarios for the ASEAN/Southeast Asia region would be most appropriate for Vietnam-specific analysis.
  • Financial Assets: PACTA for Banks covers loans only (drawn or committed amount). Other assets like equity or bonds are covered by PACTA for Investors.

Recommended Next Steps

  1. Replace demo data: Prepare a real Vietnamese bank loanbook in the required format (see data dictionary in Section 2)
  2. Source ABCD data: Investigate Asset Impact or compile Vietnamese company production profiles from regulatory filings
  3. VSIC mapping: Build a VSIC-to-PACTA sector classification lookup table
  4. Production scenarios: Source IEA WEO or NGFS scenarios for Vietnam/ASEAN region
  5. Improve steel coverage: Manually review unmatched steel borrowers; add intermediate parent names
  6. Extend sectors: Add oil & gas and aviation if relevant to portfolio
  7. Company engagement: Use the company-level results (37,349 rows) to identify specific companies driving misalignment
  8. Monitoring framework: Establish quarterly or semi-annual re-runs to track alignment improvement over time

Caveats & Limitations

  1. Demo data only: Both the loanbook and scenario data are synthetic samples. Results do not reflect any real institution’s portfolio.
  2. Scenario limitations: demo_2020 is illustrative. A real analysis requires IEA WEO, NGFS, or other authoritative scenarios.
  3. Match coverage varies: Steel at ~4% cannot support conclusions. Cement at ~62% is usable but imperfect.
  4. Power data gaps: Most power technologies lack projected production beyond the base year.
  5. Point-in-time assessment: Alignment at 2025 does not preclude convergence by 2030 or 2040.
  6. No portfolio weighting shown: Absolute production numbers do not directly reflect financial exposure magnitudes.
